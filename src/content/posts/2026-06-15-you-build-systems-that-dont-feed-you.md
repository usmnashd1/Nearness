# You Build Systems That Don’t Feed You

You call it research.

You tell yourself it is the future—agentic AI, generative systems, autonomous pipelines that learn, adapt, evolve.

But most days you are not building intelligence.

You are building hope with no funding.

You are sitting in an office with no floor beneath it,  
a house with no walls,  
and a roof made entirely of doubt.

You connect agents to websites.

You scrape data.

You download datasets no one explains to you properly.

You tell yourself you will train models.

But you do not always know what is training you back.

How many meals do you eat in a week?

You do not count.

How many hours do you sleep without thinking about parameters, weights, loss curves, broken pipelines?

You do not remember.

They told you AI is the future.

They did not tell you that you would become its unpaid architect.

You inflate systems.

You inflate datasets.

You inflate expectations.

And somewhere inside that inflation, your own sense of self expands in the wrong direction—until it is too large to fit inside anything real.

You sit in rooms full of code,  
but your life feels like a missing dataset.

No bioinformatics for your exhaustion.

No label for your fatigue.

No training set for your hunger.

Only broken dreams moving like wind through empty corridors.

You try again.

You always try again.

Because that is what you were taught.

But behind your inflated sense of grandeur,  
there are classrooms that are not as empty as your eyes.

And a belly that still remembers bread.

There is no fee.

There is no food.

There is no life in the system you are building.

Only the quiet realization that you are training machines to recognize patterns  
while your own pattern of survival is collapsing silently in the background.
